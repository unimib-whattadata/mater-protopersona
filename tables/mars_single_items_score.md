# MARS-Based Comparative Overview (Quantitative Synthesis)

*Scores represent the mean of two independent reviewers (Likert scale 1-5).*

## Overview

This table reports the quantitative evaluation of four pregnancy-related mobile applications based on the adapted MARS framework. Scores reflect the average ratings assigned by two independent reviewers across core MARS domains and additional domain-specific criteria relevant to the MATER project.

## Results

| Feature | Gravidanza+ | Sprout Pregnancy | Baby Mam | MATER |
|--------|-------------|------------------|----------|-------|
| Engagement | 4.0 | 3.5 | 3.0 | 4.5 |
| Functionality | 4.5 | 4.0 | 3.0 | 4.0 |
| Aesthetics | 5.0 | 5.0 | 3.5 | 3.5 |
| Information quality | 3.0 | 2.0 | 3.0 | 4.0 |
| Evidence traceability | 2.0 | 1.0 | 2.0 | 3.0 |
| Nutritional personalisation | 1.0 | 1.0 | 2.0 | 4.5 |
| GWG monitoring robustness | 2.0 | 1.0 | 1.0 | 4.0 |
| Privacy transparency | 3.5 | 3.0 | 3.0 | 4.0 |

## Notes

- Evaluation based on a 5-point Likert scale (1 = very poor, 5 = excellent)
- Two independent reviewers conducted the assessment
- Inter-rater discrepancies were ≤1 point and resolved through consensus
- Scores represent domain-level aggregation of item-level MARS responses

## Interpretation

MATER achieved the strongest performance in clinically relevant dimensions, particularly in nutritional personalisation and gestational weight monitoring, reflecting its rule-based adaptation logic and integration of guideline-informed feedback. While evidence traceability was rated moderately due to partial explicit citation of sources within the interface, overall results confirm improved clinical alignment compared to existing commercial applications, which primarily emphasise usability and engagement rather than structured medical guidance.
